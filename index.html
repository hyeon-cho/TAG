TAG: Tangential Amplifying Guidance for Hallucination-Resistant Diffusion Sampling

Hyunmin Cho1,*, Donghoon Ahn2,*, Susung Hong3,*, Jee Eun Kim1,
Seungryong Kim4,†, Kyong Hwan Jin1,†,
1Korea University, 2University of California, Berkeley, 3University of Washington, 4KAIST AI
*co-first authors, †corresponding authors


Abstract

Recent diffusion models achieve the state-of-the-art performance in image generation, but often suffer from semantic inconsistencies or hallucinations. While various inference-time guidance methods can enhance generation, they often operate indirectly by relying on external signals or architectural modifications, which introduces additional computational overhead.

In this paper, we propose Tangential Amplifying Guidance (TAG), a more efficient and direct guidance method that operates solely on trajectory signals without modifying the underlying diffusion model. TAG leverages an intermediate sample as a projection basis and amplifies the tangential components of the estimated scores with respect to this basis to correct the sampling trajectory. We formalize this guidance process by leveraging a first-order Taylor expansion, which demonstrates that amplifying the tangential component steers the state toward higher-probability regions, thereby reducing inconsistencies and enhancing sample quality.

TAG is a plug-and-play, architecture-agnostic module that improves diffusion sampling fidelity with minimal computational addition, offering a new perspective on diffusion guidance.

Conceptual Visualization of TAG
While standard sampling produces hallucinations by drifting into low-probability areas, TAG steers the trajectory toward high-probability regions to ensure coherent results.

What is implied by “Tangential”?
The original update \(\Delta_k:=\boldsymbol{x}_{k-1}-\boldsymbol{x}_{k}\) contains a mix of structural information and noise. We decompose this update into a normal component \(P_{k}\Delta_k\) and a tangential component \(P^{\perp}_{k}\Delta_k\).

\(\circ\) Normal component(second row) is largely unstructured and noisy.
\(\circ\) Tangential component(top row) clearly preserves the primary semantic structure of the image.


\(\implies\) By emphasizing the semantically rich tangential component, our method guides the model toward a higher-quality sample with significantly improved clarity and coherence.
